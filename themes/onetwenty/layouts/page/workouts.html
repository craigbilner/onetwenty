our workouts

Below you'll find a list of some of the techniques we use in our programmes. If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to ask one of our trainers.
  • Cardiovascular - increasing endurance in variety of exercises
  • Strength - increasing muscle endurance and power
  • Free weights - using only free wights to teach body to control external objects.
  • Body weight - increasing a persons ability to control and strengthen their movement using only their body weight
  • Gymnastic - Similar to body weight training but though the application of gymnastic equipment
  • Skill - increasing your knowledge, understanding and appreciation of movement. When we know how to move in many different ways we can then apply more weight or add speed
  • Coordination - improving the brain to work better with the body during exercise
  • Balanced - enhanced sense of stability
  • Mobility - free up your entire body, resulting in cleaner movement and more access to your muscles
  • Mixed - puts together lots of different fitness and training styles on this list
  • HIIT - High Intense Interval Training
  • Tabata - using all types of exercise a tabata will get you to work for 20 seconds and rest 10 seconds for 8 rounds.
  • Partner - working with someone else to achieve a specific task, workout or challenge.
  • Team - working in a group of 3 or more to achieve a specific task, workout or challenge.
  • Individual - Working on an area of fitness specific to you
  • Upper body - workouts that increase performance of muscle that arena the upper body
  • Lower body - workouts that increase performance of muscle that arena the lower body
  • Core - workouts that increase performance of muscle that are central to the body and help stabile the spine
  • Baseline 1 (cardiovascular specific) - These are done every 120 days (4 months) to test improvements you are making
  • Baseline 2 (strength specific) - These are done every 120 days (4 months) to test improvements
